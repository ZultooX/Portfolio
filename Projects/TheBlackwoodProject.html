The Blackwood Project
The Blackwood Project was our last project at TGA and was an amazing game to work on. Its was extremely fun to work on and this project all being together in one room and working for 16 weeks on fulltime. The reference game was Mistery, a top down 3D zombie survival murder mystery shooter game. Too many genres for a game.

Lightup Visability Mechanic

The main mechanic for our horror game was enemies and interactables become visible only in light. To make things more interesting all kinds of lights will make things visible. Point lights, Directional light and Spot lights.

First thing made was the Flashlight. It used Spotlight and making anything in dark invisible by discarding pixels.
After this we have to only affect Items and enemies by the lights. The line was way to prominent so a gradient of opacity wa inserted and it looked alot like what we wanted for the game.
An issue with this whole thing was that when doing a top down game you want to pretend like the game is 2D because thats the only way you can shoot. So now its instead a slice of cake that gets lit up and looks way better.

By default this looked not quite right, its stil a hard cut with the blur and the same all the way. What looked better was counting the color stength into the operation and made the switch more random and noisy.

Pointlight and Spotlight shadows

Now that all lights affect monsters if there is something blocking the enemy it will still be lit upp. Reason being, all of it are just a math operation checking if the pixel face is looking at the light. Same goes with point lights that needs to check for walls.

Spotlight shadows were the simplest being normal Directional shadows but as if its camputed from a camera with a lens.
Point lights its alot different, they have to shade 360 degrees around them. This is accomplished by renderig a shadow map to 6 faces of a cube texture with a 90 degree FOV to capture everything around the light. When rendering the light it checks all 6 sides of the map to see if the pixel is in shadow.
