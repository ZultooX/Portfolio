Spite: Parasite
Spite has been an amazing game to work on because spite was made completely in a custom engine. We developed this engine we called Tre Rader Kod (TRK) in 8 weeks and Spite in 10 weeks. Our reference game for this project was Diablo III and the monk class.

Physics System

Creating you own physics system is an unneccesary place to spend time on when many good ones are out there. Our physics system of choice was PhysX because its many features, big comunity and good documentation. Implementing it was quite simple at first but it was a bit crude.
Everything had a rigid body even the objects that could not move, i did not like this. I changed the implementation to not include rigid body if its just static. The system became more optimised and in general cleaner than before.

Component system

In an earlier project that i worked on from our graphics engine course, I had made a component system to make things easier for myself. It was well tought through system and thus a perfect addition for the groups game engine. The component system use a combination of both Entity Component System and Component System.

I take the updating and storing of components from the ECS and the simple way default CS handles gameobjects and components. Doing it his way removes the headache of managing gameobjects as ids that can easily become cumbersum.

Scene Exporter and Scene Loader

Our engine had no editor in it, so we had more time to spent on more important things. We instead got a resource from the TGA educators that was suppose to work out off the box, it did not. We had to modify the tool alot to make it work.

One change was exporting position, Rotation and Scale as vectors instead of one transform matrix. This exports less data and no right hand to left hand coordinates calculations.
I will say this was one of the hardest part of this whole project with multiple developers trying.

Player Attack

At first I simply put an "attack" collider, a sphere around the player and registered things that went in and out. It had many issues, I then switched to a sphere cast so no registering was needed and worked directly and way better

Closing Thoughts

Making our own engine was hard but incredibly fun to be able to do whatever we wanted and using any systems we wanted. It was nice to have about 8 weeks to just spend on our engine to perfect it before having to make a game in it.
