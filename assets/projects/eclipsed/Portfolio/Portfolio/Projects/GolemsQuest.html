Golems Quest: Crystal Keep
The fourth project at The Game Assembly was a 3D top down game made in TGE, A custom engine created and maintained by the educators at TGA. It was made during a 8 week half time. Our reference game was At Deaths Door.

Shadows

The main and major feature that I implemented was level shadows. We knew the game needed shadows half way through development when the scene looked wrong and way too bright everywhere. We had not had the lecture about shadows so I was reluctant but decided it will be worth the time time spent on it. What helped me a ton was Shadow Mapping on LearnOpenGL, and translating it to DX11 for our game

First result did not go as planned like it usually does.
It looked very weird and took a while to find what it was. In the end it was simply that the sun was looking from the bottom upwards, I rotated it by 180 degrees and the shadows displayed correctly.

Last thing that i did was to apply a procent close filtering to blur the shadow edges and make it look more interesting.

Enemy Damage Shader

This was an interesting task that i took because i had not done much shaders before. So i had to learn HLSL while doing it. Tutorials helped and using some voronoi to randomize what direction the "cracks" form.

Closing Thoughts

This project has been the hardest to this point because we had to modify our engine quite a lot because many things were broken. I really liked doing this, working more on low level development. I will say the ememy shaders could be improved alot by someone who knew more what they were doing.
